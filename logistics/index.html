Logistics

AIMOS 2025 will be held at the University of Sydney (USyd) Camperdown campus in Sydney, the capital of New South Wales.

The main conference building will be the Charles Perkins Centre (John Hopkins Dr, Camperdown NSW 2050).

Some breakout sessions may take place in the nearby Susan Wakil Health Building.

Wi-Fi

Complimentary Wi-Fi will be available throughout the conference. Login details will be available at the conference.

Accessibility

We aim to make AIMOS 2025 an accessible conference for all, and this includes our social events. As an attendee, you play an important role in this. For example, we can all commit to using a microphone when we ask questions, so that everyone can hear. We will endeavour to make microphones easily available.

Disability parking is available for those with a valid NSW Mobility Parking Scheme permit. You can also park for free in any numbered parking bay, other than those marked with restrictions such as 'University Owned Vehicles Only' or 'Loading Zone'. To find locations of disability parking spaces, visit Sydney maps and seach for 'Parking - accessible'. Information of building accessibility is available from the same site.

For more information on making your way around campus, please see here.

Getting to Sydney

Flights: Sydney has a major international airport (code SYD). You can see all the direct flights to Sydney here. From Sydney airport, there is a local train that can take you directly into the city.

Driving: You can use this site to find the driving distances and times between Sydney and any place in Australia.

Public transport

There are a number of buses that go to the Univeristy of Sydney or the nearby Royal Prince Alfred hospital on Missenden Road. Plan your journey to and from campus with trip planner. All public transport in Sydney operates on a contactless payment system which can be used with American Express, Mastercard and Visa credit or debit cards, so it is not necessary for visitors to purchase a local Opal travel card.

Bicycle parking

There are two bicycle racks located outside the Charles Perkins Centre. Ensure to secure your bike by locking the frame and both wheels of your bicycle to a hoop using heavy duty lock or chain.

Car parking

Parking is available on the Camperdown/Darlington Campus for staff, students and visitors. There are several carpark buildings, outdoor parking areas, and off-street parking. The University is a paid parking area at all times. Pay As You Go parking zones can be found using MazeMap and selecting the 'Car parking' icon. Casual parking is available at a rate of $4 per hour, up to a maximum charge of $24 per day. Download the CellOPark app onto your mobile phone and add register your vehicle licence plate and credit card to pay for parking. There are a limited number of parking spaces, so please arrive early to secure a parking spot.

Coffee on campus

The Grandstand is in the nearby Bruce Williams Pavilion; there are also a number of cafes nearby on Missenden Road.

Visa to enter Australia

If you're coming from overseas then you will need a visa to enter Australia. This will most likely be an eVisitor visa, which is free and allows you to attend a conference. You can check what visa is best for you here. Please get in touch if you need a letter of support.

Places to stay

To stay close to the conference venue, look for places in the suburbs of Camperdown, Newtown or Annandale.

Weather

According to the Bureau of Meteorology the average November temperatures in Sydney range from 18° to 26° celsius. The seven-day bureau forecast is here.
