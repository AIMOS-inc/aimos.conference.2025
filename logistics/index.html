Logistics

AIMOS 2025 will be held at the University of Sydney (USyd) Camperdown campus in Sydney, the capital of New South Wales.

The main conference building will be the Charles Perkins Centre, Charles Perkins Centre (John Hopkins Dr, Camperdown NSW 2050).

TBC: Some hackathons and workshops may take place in the nearby Susan Wakhil Health Building.

Wi-Fi

Complimentary Wi-Fi will be available throughout the conference. Login details will be available at the conference.

Accessibility

We aim to make AIMOS2025 an accessible conference for all, and this includes our social events. As an attendee, you play an important role in this. For example, we can all commit to using a microphone when we ask questions, so that everyone can hear. We will endeavour to make microphones easily available.

For more information on making your way around campus, please see here.

Getting to Sydney

Flights: Sydney has a major international airport (code SYD). You can see all the direct flights to Sydney here. From Sydney airport, there is a local train that can take you directly into the city.

Driving: You can use this site to find the driving distances and times between Sydney and any place in Australia.

Public transport

There are a number of buses that go to the Univeristy of Sydney or the nearby Royal Prince Alfred hospital on Missendon Rd. Plan your journey to and from campus with trip planner. All public transport in Sydney operates on a contactless payment system which can be used with American Express, Mastercard and Visa credit or debit cards, so it is not necessary for visitors to purchase a local Opal travel card.

Car parking

Parking is available on the Camperdown/Darlington Campus for staff, students and visitors. There are several carpark buildings, outdoor parking areas, and off-street parking. The University is a paid parking area at all times. Pay As You Go parking zones can be found using MazeMap and selecting the 'Car parking' icon. There are a limited number of parking spaces, so please arrive early to secure a parking spot.

Coffee on campus

Taste Baguette is next to the Charles perkins Centre (pictured above); The grandstand is in the Bruce Williams Pavilion; and The Gods cafe and bar is on the ground floor of the Hedley Bull building right next to the conference rooms.

Visa to enter Australia

If you're coming from overseas then you will need a visa to enter Australia. This will most likely be an eVisitor visa, which is free and allows you to attend a conference. You can check what visa is best for you here. Please get in touch if you need a letter of support.

Places to stay

To stay close to the conference venue, look for places in the suburbs of Camperdown, Newtown or Annandale.

Weather

According to the Bureau of Meteorology the average November temperatures in Sydney range from 18° to 26° celsius. The seven-day bureau forecast is here.
